Welcome To My Website!

Hello, My name is Nichole Badraun and I was diagnosed with Type 1 diabetes at 22 years old. It was such a scary time for me. I had so many new fears, “Am I going to be able to afford the medical care I need?” “Will people stare at my CGM and insulin pump?” “What if I have a low at work?” My future suddenly seemed so uncertain, and my biggest worry was, “Will I be able to have children?” At first I didn’t get a clear answer. My first endocrinologist assured me that, once I got my diabetes under control, a healthy pregnancy would be attainable. My primary care doctor however cautioned against it, saying it would be too risky for my health. The gynecologist I was using at the time terrified me with stories of diabetic women giving birth to giant babies with heart defects. Let me assure you, while it is hard work, pregnancy and a healthy baby are possible with type 1 diabetes. As I slowly adjusted to my new life of carb counting and needles, I started to do more research about Type 1 diabetes and pregnancy. This website is some of that research as well as resources and recipes. Please use what you find useful and disregard what you don’t. I had a relatively uncomplicated pregnancy in 2020 and my beautiful healthy daughter Sloane was born in 2021. I’m in no way an expert, and please consult your doctor before making any changes to your healthcare plan.

So, you are considering starting a family. As a type 1 diabetic it is best to have tight blood sugar control for around 3 months before conception. So, this is a great time to assemble a health team that you are comfortable with! A stellar endo and OBGYN are a must, but you will probably have to meet with a high risk OB at some point. And all of this can be pretty stressful so having access to a mental health professional could be helpful. During preconception another good step would be to make sure your overall health, not just your blood sugar, is in order. And upgrade for tech, if you are considering a CGM or an insulin pump, see if those options are available to you and get into your new routine.

    Questions for your OBGYN

  • Have you had patients with type 1 diabetes before?
  • Do you have a high risk OBGYN that you work with?
  • Can I have a prescription for prenatal vitamins?
  • Am I at a healthy weight for pregnancy

    Questions for your Endo

  • Can you run a blood test to check my thyroid function?
  • What is my A1c?
  • Can we run a urine test on my kidney function?
  • Will you measure my cholesterol?

Myth: Being on your period can cause your blood sugar to run high.

Fact: All stages of your menstrual cycle can cause your blood sugars to fluctuate. Tracking these changes can actually help you predict when you ovulate.

Recipe - Low Carb Energy Bites

INGREDIENTS: flax meal, pumpkin seeds, sesame seeds, sunflower seeds, ⅔ cup coconut oil, 1⅓ cup almond butter, 6 tbsp collagen powder, ½ tsp salt, ½ tsp vanilla extract, Dark chocolate chips (to taste)

INSTRUCTIONS: Mix all ingredients in the food processor! Let the mixture solidify in the fridge for 1 hour. Use hands or spoon to create 28 balls (one for each day of your cycle). Store in the fridge till time to enjoy!
